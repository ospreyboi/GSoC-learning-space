# Motivation

## Who I am
I'm Akshaj Tiwari, a first year Electronics and Computer Engineering 
student at Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology, Patiala.

## Why Mesa
I started my open source journey contributing to Accord Project, fixing 
bugs and improving documentation. It was a genuine learning experience, 
but I realized I wanted to contribute to something more directly aligned 
with where I want to go — understanding how AI systems are built and how 
the people building them think.

Mesa sits at that intersection. It's relatively new, actively evolving, 
and the problems being solved here are real. I don't want to watch from 
the sidelines I want to contribute in building it.
when my first PR at MESA-llm was merged in mesa-llm (#258) which improved error 
handling for rate-limited API calls. That experience showed me how much 
I could learn by actually digging into the codebase rather than just 
reading about it.

## What I want to learn
I want to learn how to contribute more effectively to open source not 
just fixing surface level issues but understanding systems deeply enough 
to improve them meaningfully. I'm currently learning about APIs and RAG 
(Retrieval Augmented Generation) and want to build on that here.

More than technical skills, I want to understand how people in this 
industry think about and solve problems.

## Where I want to go
After GSoC I want to keep contributing to Mesa it's new enough that 
there's still real room to shape it. I also want to build my own 
projects using what I learn here, focused on AI systems that have 
genuine real world impact.

My uncle is a Senior Legal Counsel at SAP Singapore and conversations with 
him about how legal contracts work in enterprise software is part of what 
got me into smart contract tooling initially. That same curiosity about 
where AI meets real professional workflows is what drives me here too.
